Renesh Bedre, Ph.D.

About Me

I earned Ph.D. from Louisiana State University at Baton Rouge, USA in August 2016. Currently, i am working as research associate at Texas A&M University. My primary research interest concerns about transcriptomics and genomics data analysis and, development of a Bioinformatics and data analysis tools for a large datasets in a high-throughput manner. During My Ph.D., I have earned experience on NGS data analysis, high performance computing, bioinformatics software development, web development and database management.

I am working on several Bioinformatics projects, from small to large scale sequence analysis to address abiotic and biotic stresses in plants (Cotton, Sugarcane, Rice, Potato, Tomato, Spartina etc.). I have largely applied Next generation sequencing (NGS) RNA-seq approach to study molecular mechanisms and transcriptional regulation under various abiotic and biotic stresses in a model and non-model plant species. Additionally, I have developed algorithms and software pipeline for efficient analysis for large scale biological data generated from NGS instruments.

Research Interests

  • Development of new computational methodologies for NGS analysis
  • High Performance Computing for big data analysis
  • Parallel computing (shared and distributed) for Bioinformatics tools development

Things I work on

  • Analyze large molecular datasets, especially RNA-Seq datasets generated from NGS platforms such as Illumina and Roche/454 in plants to address basic research questions
  • Develop and apply bioinformatics data analysis pipelines in order to analyze large omics data sets
  • Apply high performance computing (HPC) to analyze large NGS datasets in a high throughput manner
  • Apply statistical methods to analyze omics and microarray datasets in Python, R and Bioconductor (eg. gene expression, GO and pathway enrichment, PCA etc.
  • Write novel algorithms and implement it as per scientific needs and research goals
  • Sequence data submission at NCBI SRA and EST databases
  • Creative visualization of the data analysis results using various open-source tools such as R, matplotlib, Circos, IGV, Cytoscape etc.
  • Create databases for storing NGS data analysis results and maintain them
  • Collaborate with other research groups/scientists and perform the analysis of their data as per requirement
  • Communicate research findings through publications, oral presentations, reports and conferences
  • Review scientific manuscripts
  • Help other students and faculty to troubleshoot Bioinformatics problems (eg. Installing Linux/Unix, command line tools, Operating Linux/Unix machine, writing code in Python and Perl, debug the code, recommend statistical methods, database handling etc.)
